# 📊 Sales Performance Analysis

## 🧾 Overview
This project showcases a **Sales Performance Analysis Dashboard** developed after a comprehensive **data cleaning and preparation process using Microsoft Excel**. The objective is to transform raw sales data into meaningful insights that support strategic decision-making.

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning Process (Excel)

The dataset was cleaned and structured in **Microsoft Excel** using the following steps:

- **Initial Data Audit**
  - Reviewed the dataset for inconsistencies, errors, and missing values

- **Handling Missing Data**
  - Treated blank or null fields appropriately (removal or imputation)

- **Data Standardization**
  - Unified date formats, numerical values, and naming conventions
  - Ensured consistency across categories such as buyers, managers, and locations

- **Duplicate Removal**
  - Identified and removed duplicate records to maintain data integrity

- **Data Transformation**
  - Created calculated fields and metrics such as:
    - Total Sales
    - Transaction Count
    - Average Sales per Manager
  - Structured data for Pivot Table analysis

- **Validation & Quality Checks**
  - Verified totals, subtotals, and relationships across fields

## 📊 Dashboard Insights

### 🔹 Top 5 Buyers (Yearly Performance)
- Consistent top buyers identified across multiple years
- Highlights key revenue contributors

### 🔹 Top Suburb Groups (2015–2017)
- Sales concentration observed in specific regions
- Wagga Wagga and Chatswood show strong performance growth

### 🔹 Best-Selling Categories
- Certain product categories dominate across all years
- Clear trend of sustained high performers

### 🔹 Top Performing Managers
- Identifies managers driving the most revenue
- Useful for performance benchmarking and incentives

### 🔹 Best Selling Days
- Peak sales occur mid-week (Tuesday–Thursday)
- Lower performance observed at the start and end of the week

### 🔹 Best Selling Months
- Sales trend shows gradual decline across the year
- Early months (Jan–Mar) outperform later months

---

## 📌 Key Takeaways
- A small group of buyers and managers contribute significantly to revenue
- Regional performance varies, indicating opportunities for targeted strategies
- Mid-week sales optimization can drive higher revenue
- Seasonal trends should inform planning and forecasting
